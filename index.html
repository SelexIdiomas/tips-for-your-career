Choosing Accounting Software

What really matters when evaluating a system?

As an accounting system provider, we are often asked: “What is the most important feature to look for when evaluating accounting software?” In our opinion, the development capability of the package is the prime feature to consider.

The real value of accounting software lies not only in what it does today, but in what it can become.

Accounting software publishers generally follow a strategy of producing software that contains all of the most popular and widely used features straight out of the box. These products are designed to meet 80 to 85 per cent of the needs of all businesses.

After that, customers and their supporting dealers are expected to use the product’s in-built development tools to extend functionality and meet the remaining needs.

Modern accounting systems are built with flexibility in mind — allowing businesses to adapt the software instead of adapting to the software.

This approach has worked well since the early 1980s when Sage released one of the first truly amendable systems. Today, nearly all major accounting software providers follow this model to appeal to a wider range of industries.

In the past, customization required modifying the source code — a slow and expensive process that could take months and made future upgrades nearly impossible.

As a result, many companies became stuck using outdated versions of their software, unable to evolve with new releases.

Today’s development tools eliminate that limitation, enabling upgrades without losing custom features.

Modern systems are designed with both stability and adaptability in mind: a solid core combined with tools that allow developers to tailor the solution to specific business needs.

This keeps the system clean, efficient, and user-friendly, while still offering powerful customization when needed.

So, if you are in the market for an accounting software solution, it is essential to evaluate not just its current features, but its development capabilities as well.
